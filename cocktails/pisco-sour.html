    <h1>Pisco Sour</h1>

    <ul>

      <li>50ml Pisco</li>

      <li>25ml Fresh Lime Juice</li>

      <li>20ml Sugar Syrup (1:1)</li>

      <li>1 Egg White</li>

      <li>3 dashes Angostura Bitters (on foam)</li>

    </ul>


    <h2>Method</h2>

    <p>Dry shake (without ice) to emulsify, then shake again with ice. Double strain into a chilled coupette. Garnish with Angostura drops on the foam.</p>


    <h2>Pro Tip</h2>

    <p>Use a Peruvian pisco like Quebranta for authenticity. Egg white creates the silky texture and foamy cap. Try a 'reverse dry shake' for extra fluff.</p>


    <h2>Did You Know?</h2>

    <p>The Pisco Sour is Peru’s national drink, dating back to the early 20th century. It was created by American bartender Victor Vaughen Morris in Lima.</p>
