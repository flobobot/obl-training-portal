    <h1>Amaretto Sour</h1>

    

    <section>

      <h2>Ingredients</h2>

      <ul>

        <li>50ml Amaretto</li>

        <li>25ml Lemon Juice</li>

        <li>15ml Sugar Syrup</li>

        <li>15ml Egg White (or aquafaba)</li>

        <li>Angostura Bitters (dash on top)</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <section>

      <h2>Method</h2>

      <p>Dry shake all ingredients without ice to emulsify egg white. Add ice and shake again until well chilled. Fine strain into a rocks glass over ice. Garnish with a dash of bitters on top of the foam, optionally with a lemon wheel or cherry.</p>

    </section>


    <section>

      <h2>Pro Tips</h2>

      <ul>

        <li>Use high-quality Amaretto like Disaronno for a richer flavour.</li>

        <li>Aquafaba is a great vegan alternative to egg white, but always strain it properly.</li>

        <li>Double-shaking helps achieve a silky foam.</li>

      </ul>

    </section>


    <section>

      <h2>Did You Know?</h2>

      <p>Despite its sweetness, the Amaretto Sour became a bartender favourite after Jeffrey Morgenthaler’s modern version added bourbon to balance it — though this house version stays truer to the classic.</p>

    </section>
