    <h1>Sazerac</h1>

    <ul>

      <li>50ml Rye Whiskey</li>

      <li>1 sugar cube</li>

      <li>3 dashes Peychaud’s Bitters</li>

      <li>Absinthe (to rinse)</li>

    </ul>


    <h2>Method</h2>

    <p>Muddle the sugar cube with bitters in a mixing glass. Add whiskey and stir with ice. Rinse a chilled rocks glass with absinthe and discard the excess. Strain the drink into the glass. Garnish with a lemon twist.</p>


    <h2>Pro Tip</h2>

    <p>Chill your glass in advance and use a dropper for the absinthe if you want precise control. This cocktail is all about balance and subtle aromatics.</p>


    <h2>Did You Know?</h2>

    <p>Born in New Orleans, the Sazerac is often called America’s first cocktail, dating back to the 1830s and originally made with Cognac before switching to rye whiskey.</p>
