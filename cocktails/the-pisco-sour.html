House Riff

The Pisco Sour

Ingredients

  • El Gobernador (apricot infused): 50ml
  • Lime Juice: 25ml
  • Simple Syrup or Gomme: 10ml
  • Egg White: 25ml
  • Orange Angostura Bitters: 1 dash

Method

Reverse dry shake, then shake with ice and fine strain into a coupette.

Glassware

Coupette

Garnish

Bitters dots or swirl on foam.

Pro Tip

Use a reverse dry shake to maximise foam without over-diluting the pisco, just a swift dash of orange bitters in the tin will make a difference that goes a long way, separating a “classic classic” into a memory - people will come back for more. Share the love.

Did You Know?

This Peruvian classic dates back to the early 1900s and is a national drink with disputed origin claims - therefore no more details about origins and disputed claims. It is, hands down, one of the most universally loved drinks, by amateurs and connoisseurs alike. It has a degree of boldness but is, simultaneously, surprisingly sophisticated.
